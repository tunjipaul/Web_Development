History of Forensics

Forensic is the study or science of solving crimes by using scientific knowledge or methods.

relating to or dealing with the application of scientific knowledge to legal problems

The noun forensic, meaning an argumentative exercise derived from the adjective forensic, whose earliest meaning in English is belonging to, used in, or suitable to courts or to public discussion and debate. The English word was derived from a Latin word forensic meaning of the market place or forum, public, which in turn comes from the Latin word forum, meaning “market place, forum.”
